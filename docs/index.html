What makes a good karaoke song?

By Khadija Alam

June 30, 2026

One of my favorite places in the city is this hole-in-the-wall karaoke spot in Koreatown. There's no sign outside the building. You have to walk down a narrow hallway and go up a small elevator to access it. It's BYOB. It's perfect.

During my most recent karaoke session with my friends, one of them put on Since U Been Gone by Kelly Clarkson as our first song. Great song! Also, incredibly difficult to sing.

Despite the fact that our voices gave out trying to hit those high notes in the bridge, we still had a blast! And it got me thinking about what makes a song good for karaoke.

Does it matter if it's got high notes that are hard to sing? Does it have to be a popular song so your other friends can join in and sing along? I wanted to see if I could use data to try and figure it out.

To find popular karaoke songs, I looked at three karaoke playlists on Spotify with over 750k followers between them and focused on the songs that appeared at least twice across the playlists.

Somehow, Mr. Brightside by The Killers and Umbrella by Rihanna and Jay-Z show up four times across the three playlists!

The pop song reigns supreme

Unsurprisingly, most of the tracks are pop songs. But pop is a very broad category! Technically, it just means "popular," but the sounds that are considered popular change over time. And many pop songs also have influences from other music genres, like R&B or disco.

In general though, a lot of pop songs share certain qualities, like familiar melodies or common chord progressions (I-V-vi-IV is a classic one, and if that means nothing to you, check out this video). They also tend to be structured similarly with a catchy hook that often repeats three times.

This works great for karaoke because even if someone doesn't know a song, they'll probably still be able to join in on the fun and sing along by the time the second or third chorus hits.

The power of nostalgia

All but one of the popular karaoke songs we're looking at were released before 2013. The outlier is Shallow by Lady Gaga and Bradley Cooper, which was released in 2018 and has become a pretty classic karaoke duet.

This could be related to why pop is the most dominant genre among popular karaoke songs. Like pop songs, older music might be more familiar and recognizable to more people. So it may just take time for a track to become a canonical karaoke song — unless you're as iconic as Gaga!

It doesn't have to be a chart hit to be a karaoke hit

The Billboard Hot 100 Year-End charts contain the songs that commercially performed the best in a given year in the US — that includes things like radio play, album sales, and streaming. Billboard's exact methodology has changed over the years as the ways that we listen to music evolve but it still gives us an idea of a song's popularity.

Some songs — Taylor Swift's Love Story, Miley Cyrus' Party in the USA, Bruno Mars' Just The Way You Are, and Adele's Some Like You — charted two years in a row. But for the most part, it seems like commercial popularity doesn't necessarily correlate with a song's popularity when it comes to karaoke.

It doesn't have to be easy to sing (but it helps if it is!)

Let's focus in on the nine songs that appear in all three karaoke playlists.

Take a look at our two super popular karaoke songs — the ones that appeared four times across the three playlists, Mr. Brightside by The Killers and Umbrella by Rihanna and Jay-Z. Both of them require the smallest vocal range (i.e., you don't have to be able to hit super high or super low notes).

Kelly Clarkson is on another planet with Since U Been Gone, which has three octaves between the lowest and highest notes.

On the other hand, Mr. Brightside is mainly comprised of just two notes in the vocal line. Plus, the melody of the verses is more similar to talking than singing so you don't even need to hit those notes exactly to still sound good singing the song.

But karaoke isn't really about singing well anyways! I think it's about community and sharing a love for music. So it makes sense that a lot of popular karaoke songs are ones that people know — pop hits that have been around for a while.

But if you're in a pinch and don't know what to sing at karaoke, just go with Mr. Brightside by The Killers.
